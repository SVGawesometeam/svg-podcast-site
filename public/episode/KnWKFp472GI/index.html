About the Guest

Dmitri Mogilko
Co-founder, Linguatrip

Dmitri Mogilko is the co-founder of Linguatrip, an edtech platform focused on language learning and study-abroad programs. He and Marina built the company from the ground up in Russia before relocating to Silicon Valley and securing a spot in the prestigious 500 Startups accelerator program. Though he and Marina stopped working together professionally in 2019, Dmitri remains her husband of eight years.

In this episode of the Silicon Valley Girl Podcast, Marina Mogilko interviews Dmitri Mogilko, Co-founder, Linguatrip. Marina Mogilko sits down with her husband and former co-founder Dmitri to discuss what it was really like to build Linguatrip together — scaling from 5 to 60 employees in under six months, getting accepted into 500 Startups, and relocating to Silicon Valley as immigrants with no network. They open up about why they made the decision to stop working together in 2019, exploring the complementary strengths and personal tensions that defined their co-founder relationship.

Key Takeaways

  • Marina and Dmitri scaled Linguatrip from 5–6 employees to 60 in less than half a year after getting into 500 Startups.
  • Some investors flagged their couple status as a red flag during fundraising, which Marina found deeply frustrating.
  • Dmitri's 'dream big' mindset — including cold-DMing Mark Zuckerberg on LinkedIn from St. Petersburg — provided the ambition that pushed them toward Silicon Valley, while Marina handled operations and product execution.
  • Not knowing enough American startup culture actually helped early on: Dmitri mistook a polite 'keep us posted' reply from a top VC as a near-done deal, and that misplaced confidence fueled six more months of momentum.
  • After nearly a decade of working together, they stopped being business partners in 2019 — a decision they are sharing publicly for the first time in this episode.
