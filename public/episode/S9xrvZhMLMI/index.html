In this episode of the Silicon Valley Girl Podcast, Marina Mogilko interviews Alex, Investor and Financial Content Creator, Ticker Symbol: YOU (YouTube). Marina Mogilko interviews Alex, a former rocket scientist who has funded his lifestyle entirely through investing for the past 10 years. They discuss whether AI stocks are in a bubble, how to evaluate companies using forward PE ratios, and Alex's concentrated portfolio strategy including 40% in Nvidia and 25% in Palantir. Alex also shares the psychological principles behind smart investing, including why behavior accounts for 90% of investment returns.

Key Takeaways

  • AI hardware and infrastructure (chips, data centers, servers) are not in a bubble — revenues and profits are already following valuations, as seen with Nvidia's earnings growth.
  • Forward PE ratios under 30 are a useful valuation benchmark, but growth-stage AI companies require looking 1–3 years out rather than at current earnings snapshots.
  • Analyst earnings estimates are frequently wrong, sometimes by a large margin — understanding a company's product and service first can help investors get ahead of the numbers.
  • Alex runs a highly concentrated portfolio with 40% in a single stock (Nvidia) and 25% in Palantir, deliberately holding no bonds, betting on high-conviction AI-era winners.
  • Investor behavior drives roughly 90% of returns — avoiding panic-selling during market downturns and being 'greedy when others are fearful' is the core psychological discipline he follows.
