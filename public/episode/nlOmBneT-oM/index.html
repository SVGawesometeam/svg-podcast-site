About the Guest

Priscilla Chan
Co-founder and Co-CEO, Chan Zuckerberg Initiative

Dr. Priscilla Chan is a physician, philanthropist, and co-founder and co-CEO of the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative, which she established alongside her husband Mark Zuckerberg. She trained as a pediatrician and has dedicated her post-clinical career to advancing science and education through large-scale philanthropic investment. CZI is at the forefront of combining frontier AI with biological research to help cure, prevent, and manage all diseases.

In this episode of the Silicon Valley Girl Podcast, Marina Mogilko interviews Priscilla Chan, Co-founder and Co-CEO, Chan Zuckerberg Initiative. Marina Mogilko interviews Dr. Priscilla Chan about how the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative is using AI and frontier science to transform medicine at the cellular level. Priscilla explains the mission behind building virtual cell models — AI systems capable of simulating biological life — and how this technology could accelerate drug discovery and enable personalized medicine. She also shares the personal clinical moment that shifted her focus from treating disease to preventing it.

Key Takeaways

  • Priscilla and Mark Zuckerberg have committed 99% of their wealth to the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative, funding efforts like Biohubs and virtual cell research.
  • CZI is building the world's first virtual cell models — AI systems that simulate life at the cellular level to speed up drug discovery and reduce clinical trial risk.
  • Rather than targeting a single disease, CZI deliberately builds tools and infrastructure for all scientists, multiplying the impact across many research areas.
  • Priscilla describes a pivotal moment in the clinic that shifted her perspective from reactive medicine to a prevention-first approach to healthcare.
  • Within the next five years, virtual cell technology could begin transforming patient care by making personalized medicine more accessible and practical.
