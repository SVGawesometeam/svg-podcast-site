About the Guest

Young Zhao
Co-founder and CEO, OpusClip

Young Zhao is the co-founder and CEO of OpusClip, an AI-powered platform that transforms long-form content such as videos, articles, and papers into short-form clips optimized for social media. He built OpusClip into one of the fastest-growing AI companies in the world, reaching 50 million users and a $215 million valuation in just two and a half years. Before finding product-market fit with OpusClip, Young navigated multiple failed product attempts, including a live-streaming tool, before pivoting around a single breakout clipping feature.

In this episode of the Silicon Valley Girl Podcast, Marina Mogilko interviews Young Zhao, Co-founder and CEO, OpusClip. Young Zhao, co-founder and CEO of OpusClip, joins Marina Mogilko to share how he built a $215M AI startup with 50 million users after multiple failed products. He walks through a practical 2026 playbook for AI founders, covering how to validate ideas before building, when to pivot, and how to find real product-market fit. The conversation also explores the future of content creation, the rise of AI agents, and what it takes for creators to stand out in an increasingly competitive landscape.

Key Takeaways

  • Engineer the result before building the product — OpusClip's team manually created and emailed AI-edited clips to potential users and received over 60% positive feedback before writing a single line of product code.
  • Use a Discord bot as an MVP — Young's team skipped UI/UX entirely and launched inside Discord first, saving significant development time while still validating core value and measuring retention.
  • Qualitative signals matter as much as metrics — when users started complaining about queue times and daily usage quotas, that frustration was the clearest sign of product-market fit.
  • Build a real business, not a cool demo — many founders polish demos that showcase impressive capabilities but never solve a genuine pain point, which is the primary reason early-stage AI startups fail to scale.
  • Every AI founder needs to be 'aged' — meaning they must stay close enough to the frontier to anticipate what large model releases will make obsolete in the next few weeks or months, and build accordingly.
