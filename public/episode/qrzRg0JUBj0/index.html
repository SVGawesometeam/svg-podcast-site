In this episode of the Silicon Valley Girl Podcast, Marina Mogilko shares Marina Mogilko, entrepreneur and investor, breaks down 13 habits that silently keep people from building wealth, drawing on her own journey from founding a study-abroad company in 2014 to generating investment income nearly equal to her business income. She covers practical financial strategies including budgeting with the 50/30/20 rule, diversifying income streams, avoiding lifestyle inflation, and automating savings. The episode provides actionable steps listeners can take immediately to course-correct their financial habits and work toward millionaire status.

Key Takeaways

  • Track every dollar using the 50/30/20 rule — 50% for essentials, 30% for wants, and 20% for savings or debt repayment — and dedicate at least a few hours per month to reviewing your budget spreadsheets.
  • Most millionaires have multiple income streams; Marina herself has 19, which allowed her business (LinguaTrip) to survive COVID because online courses offset the collapse of in-person bookings.
  • Lifestyle inflation silently erodes wealth — as income rises, unnecessary spending often rises with it, preventing savings and investment from scaling at the same rate.
  • Avoiding investing is one of the costliest habits: Marina's investment income now nearly matches her business revenue, illustrating the compounding power of putting money to work early.
  • Small financial 'leaks' — forgotten subscriptions, uninvested cash sitting in accounts, and fraudulent charges — can drain significant wealth over time and are only caught through consistent budget monitoring.

Habit number three is falling for lifestyle inflation, and we see it a lot with creators. And yes, I do that sometimes too, but not that often. Basically, lifestyle inflation happens when your expenses rise with your income. You got a raise? Suddenly you're dining out more, upgrading your car, or moving to a more expensive apartment. Another very bad thing that you can do is avoid investing. Not investing is one of the biggest missed opportunities for building wealth.
