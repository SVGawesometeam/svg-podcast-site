About the Host

Marina Mogilko
Host, Silicon Valley Girl Podcast

Entrepreneur, content creator, and founder based in Silicon Valley. Marina interviews the world's top tech leaders, investors, and innovators to uncover the trends, strategies, and mindsets shaping the future. With millions of followers across platforms, she brings a unique perspective on technology, business, and personal growth.

In this episode of the Silicon Valley Girl Podcast, Marina Mogilko shares Marina Mogilko outlines seven AI skills professionals need to master to stay competitive as automation reshapes the job market. She covers precision prompting, trend spotting, AI content strategy, localization, personalization, content creation, and critical thinking. The episode emphasizes that learning the right AI tools and workflows — not a tech degree — is what separates those who thrive from those who get replaced.

Key Takeaways

  • Precision prompting is now a billable skill — freelancers using tools like Claude, EasyGen.io, and Poppy AI earn $80–$200/hour by producing 4–5 high-quality LinkedIn posts per hour instead of one.
  • Trend spotting has shifted from reactive to predictive — Marina's COO uploads 30–50 creator economy newsletters into ChatGPT to identify emerging industry trends before they go mainstream.
  • Trend reports command real money: quick reports sell for $50–$100, in-depth analyses for $300–$500, and exclusive trend insights for up to $1,000.
  • By 2030, 85 million jobs could be displaced by automation, yet 92% of companies plan to double their AI usage by 2026 — making AI fluency a near-mandatory career skill.
  • Critical thinking and an entrepreneurial mindset is the seventh and most durable skill — Marina considers it so foundational she is already teaching it to her children, ages 3 and 5.
