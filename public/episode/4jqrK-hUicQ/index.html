About the Guest

Cassey Ho
Founder & CEO, Blogilates / Pop Flex Active

Cassey Ho is the fitness influencer, entrepreneur, and designer behind Blogilates, one of the most-followed fitness channels on YouTube. She is the founder of Pop Flex Active, a direct-to-consumer apparel brand known for innovative activewear, swimwear, and lifestyle clothing, and the creator of the Blogilates brand sold exclusively at Target across all 1,800 U.S. stores. Known for turning a loyal online community into a product-driven empire, Cassey also serves as head designer across both of her apparel brands.

In this episode of the Silicon Valley Girl Podcast, Marina Mogilko interviews Cassey Ho, Founder & CEO, Blogilates / Pop Flex Active. Cassey Ho joins Marina Mogilko to discuss how she built two multi-million dollar apparel brands — Pop Flex and Blogilates (exclusive to Target) — starting from YouTube workout videos for 40 people. They cover her dual-brand strategy, launching 1.1 million units at 1,800 Target stores, AI theft of her content, burnout, and why over 99% of her revenue now comes from apparel rather than ads or brand deals. The conversation offers a candid behind-the-scenes look at what it takes to build a lasting creator-founded brand in 2025.

Key Takeaways

  • Launching at Target with Blogilates actually increased Pop Flex revenue rather than cannibalizing it — with 90% of inventory in 3 key styles selling out within 3 days of launch.
  • Over 99% of Cassey's business revenue comes from apparel sales; less than 1% comes from ad revenue or brand deals, a dramatic shift from a typical creator monetization model.
  • Pop Flex serves as the innovation lab — new products are tested there first, then redesigned at a lower price point for the Target/Blogilates line, creating a clear two-tier product strategy.
  • To fulfill the Target launch, Cassey's team had to manufacture 1.1 million units in under a year with no prior experience at that scale, which she describes as 'winning the retail Super Bowl.'
  • AI-driven content theft is a serious ongoing issue for Cassey — bad actors have stolen her videos and used AI to replace her face, prompting her to commit to fighting these violations on principle regardless of cost.
