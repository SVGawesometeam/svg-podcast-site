About the Guest

Oscar Hoglund

Oscar Hoglund is the co-founder and CEO of Epidemic Sound, a Stockholm-based music licensing platform valued at $1.4 billion. Under his leadership, Epidemic Sound has become one of the world's leading music solutions for content creators, with its catalog soundtracking billions of daily video views across major platforms. Oscar is also an active voice on AI's role in democratizing creative tools for founders and creators.

In this episode of the Silicon Valley Girl Podcast, Marina Mogilko interviews Oscar Hoglund, Co-founder and CEO, Epidemic Sound. He also opens up about his personal breaking point as a CEO and how he rebuilt his approach to leadership and life balance.

Key Takeaways

  • Epidemic Sound soundtracks content generating 3 billion views per day on YouTube alone, giving Oscar rare data-driven insight into what makes content resonate emotionally and go viral.
  • The 'CROSS' strategy — rooted in cognitive dissonance — explains why audiences are drawn to unexpected combinations, such as a sports star discussing addiction rather than athletic success.
  • Understanding your value chain is critical: Oscar defines leverage as achieving a large output from a small input, and says it was one of the most important frameworks Epidemic Sound ever applied.
  • The 'Three WHY' framework helps founders avoid rushing into AI products by forcing them to dig beneath surface-level problems before building solutions.
  • Oscar's wife telling him 'our life is better when you're not here' was his personal breaking point, prompting him to fundamentally restructure how he operates as a CEO and maintain his sanity.
