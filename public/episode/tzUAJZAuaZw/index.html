About the Guest

Raj Gokal
Co-Founder, Solana

Raj Gokal is the co-founder of Solana, a high-performance blockchain platform with a peak valuation exceeding $83 billion. Before Solana, he spent nearly a decade founding and co-founding companies in the health-tech space, including roles at Omada Health. He is known for his focus on rebuilding large, regulated industries through decentralized technology.

In this episode of the Silicon Valley Girl Podcast, Marina Mogilko interviews Raj Gokal, Co-Founder, Solana. Marina Mogilko interviews Raj Gokal, co-founder of Solana, about the mindset and decisions that led him to build one of the world's most valuable blockchain platforms. Raj shares his journey from failed health-tech startups to discovering crypto, finding co-founder Anatoly Yakovenko, and giving the Solana project just 6 months before committing fully. The conversation also covers Solana's growth strategy, the crypto device Saga, NFTs as audience engagement tools, and the emerging concept of DePIN.

Key Takeaways

  • Co-founder relationship is the single most important factor in starting a company — Raj left his first startup (a glucose sensor company acquired by One Drop) partly due to a misaligned partnership dynamic.
  • Raj spent 6 years in health-tech before pivoting to crypto, concluding that healthcare incumbents like insurance companies and the FDA are too powerful to disrupt quickly without the backing of a company like Apple or Google.
  • He gave co-founder Anatoly Yakovenko only 6 months to prove Solana's concept before fully committing — a deliberate, risk-managed approach to joining a new venture.
  • Crypto's global nature diversifies regulatory and market risk: when the US clamps down, markets like Korea, Dubai, Singapore, and London compete to attract crypto startups.
  • Solana's daily trading volume runs between $1–3 billion, and the network's valuation reached $83 billion, built on the thesis that removing financial intermediaries with peer-to-peer technology is far faster than rebuilding regulated industries like healthcare.
