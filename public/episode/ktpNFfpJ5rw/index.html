About the Guest

Ryan Roslansky
CEO of LinkedIn and EVP at Microsoft

Ryan Roslansky is the CEO of LinkedIn and Executive Vice President at Microsoft, where he also oversees Microsoft Copilot and Microsoft Office. Under his leadership, LinkedIn grew from $7 billion to $17 billion in revenue and surpassed one billion members globally. He is the author of 'Open to Work,' a new book on navigating AI-driven career change.

In this episode of the Silicon Valley Girl Podcast, Marina Mogilko interviews Ryan Roslansky, CEO of LinkedIn and EVP at Microsoft. Marina Mogilko sits down with LinkedIn CEO Ryan Roslansky at Davos to discuss what LinkedIn's labor market data actually reveals about AI's impact on jobs. Roslansky breaks down why entry-level hiring is down 12%, identifies the 5 human skills AI cannot replace, and names the 3 job categories poised for explosive growth over the next five years. He also discusses his new book 'Open to Work' and explains why skills-based profiles are replacing traditional resumes.

Key Takeaways

  • AI is a net positive for jobs so far — LinkedIn data shows 1.3 million new AI-related roles and over 600,000 new data center jobs created on the platform.
  • Entry-level job hiring rates are down roughly 12%, but this decline is proportional across all job levels and is driven by macroeconomic conditions and interest rates, not AI.
  • Career ladders are giving way to horizontal skill expansion — LinkedIn's most-requested feature is career path visualization, but generalist hiring is now the dominant trend.
  • There are currently 75 million LinkedIn members who identify as creators, with 4 million listing it as their full-time job title — a sign of the creator economy's mainstream legitimacy.
  • 50% of U.S. college graduates this year are expected to be unemployed or underemployed, and student loan debt is outpacing credit card debt for the first time in history.
