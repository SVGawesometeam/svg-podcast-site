About the Guest

Jenny Lei
Founder & CEO, Freja New York

Jenny Lei is the founder and CEO of Freja New York, a direct-to-consumer vegan leather handbag brand she launched in 2020 with an initial run of 300 bags. Born in China and raised largely in the United States, Jenny built Freja without a design background, leveraging years of e-commerce and dropshipping experience to scale the company to over $9 million in revenue. She secured an EB-1 visa through her entrepreneurial achievements and has grown Freja into a brand worn by high-profile celebrities and influencers worldwide.

In this episode of the Silicon Valley Girl Podcast, Marina Mogilko interviews Jenny Lei, Founder & CEO, Freja New York. Marina Mogilko interviews Jenny Lei, the immigrant founder of Freja New York, a vegan leather handbag brand that grew from 300 bags in 2020 to over $9–10 million in annual revenue. Jenny shares how being on a visa with no path to a US passport forced her to build her own business, and how early experience drop shipping bags gave her the product and market knowledge to launch Freja. The conversation covers her rocky launch, influencer traction with names like Hailey Bieber and Matilda Djerf, and her philosophy that setbacks are part of the entrepreneurial story.

Key Takeaways

  • Jenny launched Freja with just 300 bags in 2020 after failing to land a job — her visa status meant staying in the US required generating her own income.
  • She ran 7 dropshipping stores before finding success selling bags from China, giving her concrete data on what styles, colors, and markets perform before ever designing her own product.
  • Perfecting the product took 8 months of factory back-and-forth despite having no design background — she credits 'product is king, marketing is queen' as her core philosophy.
  • Her go-to-market strategy relied heavily on paid advertising and email list building from day one, a skill carried over from her dropshipping days — the brand remains DTC-first via Shopify plus select retailers.
  • Not a single bag sold on launch day, yet the brand eventually attracted celebrity fans including Hailey Bieber, Matilda Djerf, and Katie Holmes, demonstrating how public failures can become part of a compelling brand story.
