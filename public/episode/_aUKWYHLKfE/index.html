About the Guest

Conor Grennan
Chief AI Architect, NYU Stern School of Business; Founder, AI Mindset

Conor Grennan is the Chief AI Architect at NYU Stern School of Business and the founder of AI Mindset, a training and consulting organization that works with Fortune 500 companies and business leaders. Rather than focusing on specific tools or features, his work centers on a behavioral framework for fundamentally changing how individuals and organizations think about and interact with AI. Grennan is a writer and entrepreneur who has become one of the leading voices on practical, day-to-day AI adoption.

In this episode of the Silicon Valley Girl Podcast, Marina Mogilko interviews Conor Grennan, Chief AI Architect, NYU Stern School of Business; Founder, AI Mindset. Marina Mogilko sits down with Conor Grennan, Chief AI Architect at NYU Stern School of Business, to explore why most people underutilize AI despite claiming to use it. Grennan argues the gap is behavioral, not technological, and shares practical frameworks for treating AI as a thinking companion rather than a search engine. They also discuss how hiring is shifting, with up to 30% of entry-level white-collar jobs at risk, and what skills help candidates stand out in the AI era.

Key Takeaways

  • True AI power users engage with the tool 30+ times a day across both work and home tasks — not a few queries per week — making frequency and depth the real differentiators.
  • AI should be treated as a companion, not Google: long, ongoing conversations unlock far more value than single-shot search-style prompts.
  • Write your first draft yourself before involving AI — then paste it in and ask 'How can I make this more readable?' or 'What am I missing?' to preserve your voice while improving quality.
  • Even without any further technological advancement, AI could eliminate roughly 25% of entry-level white-collar jobs; candidates who visibly reinvent workflows with AI stand out more than those who simply do more work.
  • You can build persistent AI memory in minutes by exporting context from one platform and importing it into another, ensuring your AI 'knows you' regardless of which tool you're using.
