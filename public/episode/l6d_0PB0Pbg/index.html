In this episode of the Silicon Valley Girl Podcast, Marina Mogilko interviews a special guest, . Marina Mogilko breaks down why over 100,000 tech workers have been laid off in 2024 despite AI not yet fully replacing human roles, drawing on insights from her podcast interviews with founders and CEOs. She identifies which jobs are most at risk, which skills will matter most in the AI era, and why the traditional education-to-employment pipeline is increasingly broken. The episode concludes with a 3-step plan for making oneself irreplaceable in an AI-transformed job market.

Key Takeaways

  • Over 100,000 tech workers were laid off in 2024 — companies like Amazon cut 14,000 corporate roles and Duolingo phased out human contractors in favor of AI automation.
  • The education system was built for an industrial economy, and graduates are now competing not only with AI but also with globally distributed talent armed with the same tools at lower costs.
  • Junior roles are being hit hardest first — the entry-level 'first rung' of the career ladder is disappearing, raising serious questions about how future professionals will build experience.
  • AI is disrupting knowledge-based professions like financial advisors and real estate agents — tools like Perplexity can already generate full portfolio rebalancing strategies, threatening advisors who offer only commodity information.
  • The key to staying relevant is the ability to solve ambitious problems end-to-end using AI tools, produce 10x output, and build a strong personal brand — skills no model or cheap freelancer can easily replicate together.
