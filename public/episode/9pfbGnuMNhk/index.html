In this episode of the Silicon Valley Girl Podcast, Marina Mogilko shares Marina Mogilko outlines seven future-proof skills professionals should prioritize in 2026 to remain competitive in an AI-driven job market. Drawing on stats from LinkedIn, Lightcast, and the World Economic Forum, she argues that skills like problem framing, AI literacy, and workflow orchestration are more valuable than any single tool. She features insights from Replit CEO Amjad Masad and Anthropic CPO Mike Krieger to ground the advice in real-world practice.

Key Takeaways

  • LinkedIn data shows professionals are adding new skills 140% faster since late 2022, and roles requiring AI skills pay 28% more — roughly $18,000 extra per year.
  • Problem framing is ranked the #1 skill globally through 2030 by the World Economic Forum; knowing what you're solving before opening an AI tool is a core competitive advantage.
  • Prompting is now a baseline literacy: Replit CEO Amjad Masad recommends building prompting skills by practicing on YouTube tutorials and using AI itself (e.g., GPT-5) to restructure your prompts.
  • Workflow orchestration allows a single person to operate at the output of a small team by chaining AI tools together — no deep technical background required.
  • Anthropic CPO Mike Krieger notes founders are using Claude as a product manager, lawyer, and strategic advisor, reflecting how AI is replacing entire functional roles for lean startups.
