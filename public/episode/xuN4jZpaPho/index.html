In this episode of the Silicon Valley Girl Podcast, Marina Mogilko interviews a special guest, . Marina Mogilko shares the 11 financial habits she follows every time she gets paid, drawing on her personal journey from spending impulsively on Louis Vuitton and vacations in her early blogging years to building seven-figure wealth. She covers strategies ranging from paying yourself a set salary as an entrepreneur and tracking income in a monthly spreadsheet, to using tax-advantaged accounts like a Solo 401(k) and Roth IRA. The episode emphasizes financial discipline, automating money flows, and understanding opportunity cost as the foundation for lasting wealth.

Key Takeaways

  • Small daily spending — $6 coffee, $18 takeout, $5 rideshare, $3 snacks, $2 subscriptions — silently adds up to over $11,000 per year if left unchecked.
  • Entrepreneurs should set a fixed monthly salary for themselves early on; going years without paying yourself leads to burnout and second-guessing the decision to start a business.
  • Tracking every income source and its associated costs in a monthly spreadsheet lets you quickly cut underperforming projects — such as a TikTok channel that generated zero revenue after four months.
  • Tax-advantaged accounts matter: a Solo 401(k) can reduce taxable income while building retirement wealth, and a Roth IRA allows after-tax contributions to grow and be withdrawn tax-free.
  • Automating finances — directing money to savings, investments, and taxes the moment a paycheck arrives — removes decision fatigue and makes disciplined wealth-building the default behavior.
